## Concept Introduction

AI Engineering — building applications on top of LLMs, vector databases, and
retrieval systems — is one of the fastest-growing roles in software. This
lesson maps out the skills you'll build across RAG Academy and how they
translate to real job roles. By the end of this lesson you'll have a clear
picture of the career path ahead.

## How It Works

The AI Engineer role sits between software engineering and machine learning.
You don't need a PhD or deep math background — you need to know how to build
software that uses AI models effectively. The core skills are:

1. **Foundation** (Junior tier): Python, web development, databases. You can
   build and deploy a working application.
2. **AI Literacy** (Mid tier): How LLMs work, prompt engineering, embeddings,
   basic RAG. You can integrate AI into applications.
3. **Architecture** (Senior tier): Production RAG, evaluation, security,
   advanced retrieval. You can design and optimize AI systems.
4. **Innovation** (Expert tier): Agentic RAG, multi-modal, graph-based
   retrieval. You can push the boundaries of what's possible.

Job titles at each level:
- Junior → Junior AI Engineer, Python Developer
- Mid → AI Engineer, RAG Developer
- Senior → Senior AI Engineer, AI Architect
- Expert → Staff AI Engineer, AI Researcher

## Common Pitfalls

- **Pitfall:** Trying to learn everything at once — jumping to LangChain
  before understanding Python, or to agentic RAG before basic retrieval.
  **Fix:** Follow the tier progression. Each tier builds on the previous one.
- **Pitfall:** Reading without building. Watching tutorials feels productive
  but doesn't create understanding. **Fix:** Type every code example. Build
  every exercise. The portfolio projects are the point.
- **Pitfall:** Comparing your progress to others. Some learners have years of
  programming experience; others are starting fresh. **Fix:** Measure against
  your own progress through the tiers, not against someone else's timeline.
